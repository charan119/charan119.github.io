Comparison of Exact and CoreExact Algorithms for Densest Subgraph Discovery

Performance Analysis Across Datasets and Clique Sizes

Introduction

This webpage compares the performance of two algorithms from the paper "Efficient Algorithms for Densest Subgraph Discovery" by Yixiang Fang et al.:

  • Algorithm-1 (Exact): An exact method using network flow to find the densest subgraph based on h-clique-density.
  • Algorithm-4 (CoreExact): An optimized exact algorithm leveraging core decomposition to improve efficiency while maintaining accuracy.
The comparison is based on experiments conducted on three small networks: AS733, Netscience, and Ca-HepTh, with clique sizes (h) ranging from 2 to 6. The metrics visualized include density and execution time, derived from the provided experimental results.

Insights

  • Density Trends: Across all datasets, density increases with clique size (h). Ca-HepTh shows the most dramatic rise (15.5 at h=2 to 11689.4 at h=6), indicating its potential for extremely dense subgraphs, followed by Netscience (9.5 to 1938) and AS733 (8.875 to 123.393).
  • Exact Algorithm Performance: Execution time for the Exact algorithm increases with h, particularly for larger datasets. For Ca-HepTh, it rises from 27.3634 sec (h=2) to 529.803 sec (h=6), reflecting the computational complexity of the network flow approach.
  • CoreExact Algorithm Performance: CoreExact generally outperforms Exact, especially in AS733, where time decreases from 24.19 sec (h=2) to 4.834 sec (h=6). However, in Netscience at h=6, it spikes to 210.07 sec (vs. 4.12113 sec for Exact), likely due to core decomposition overhead.
  • Time Comparison: CoreExact offers significant speedups for smaller h (e.g., 1.93x faster in AS733 at h=2), but the advantage diminishes at higher h in dense graphs like Ca-HepTh, where both algorithms converge to 529.803 sec at h=6.
  • Anomaly: The Netscience h=6 case highlights that CoreExact’s efficiency is graph-dependent, with potential inefficiencies in specific structures due to core decomposition complexity.

Conclusion

The results demonstrate CoreExact’s efficiency in reducing computation time while maintaining the same density as the Exact algorithm, making it a practical choice for densest subgraph discovery, particularly in smaller or moderately dense graphs. For further details, refer to the paper: PVLDB, 12(11): 1719-1732, 2019.
